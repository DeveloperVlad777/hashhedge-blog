Why Choosing a Prop Firm Is Harder Than It Seems
The challenge of choosing a prop trading firm is that some of them are outright scams.
A trader passes the challenge, pays the fee, receives a funded account, and starts trading profitably. Then they discover that payouts are delayed for weeks or only partially processed. Or worse: the rules change after the account has already been activated.
In prop trading, you are working with real capital. That is why choosing a prop firm requires the same level of precision as building a trading strategy.
In this article, we break down the key selection criteria, compare important conditions, and explain what to evaluate first.
Key Criteria for Choosing a Crypto Prop Firm
Not all factors are equally important. Here is what you should prioritize when choosing a crypto prop trading firm.
1
Profit Split
This is your share of the profits you generate. The higher the percentage, the more you keep from every profitable trade. Important: make sure the percentage is fixed in the terms and conditions and does not change after account activation.
2
Capital Allocation
Look at the actual account sizes available. What is the smallest account you can start with? What is the maximum account size available through scaling? The more capital under management, the higher your potential payout at the same profit percentage.
3
Risk Management Rules
Pay close attention to the daily loss limit and maximum account drawdown. These parameters should be clearly defined with exact numbers, not vague descriptions. If the rules say “no more than X% per day,” that is transparent. If the wording is unclear, consider it a warning sign.
4
Tradable Assets
Check which instruments are available. For crypto traders, access to major pairs such as BTC/USDT and ETH/USDT is essential, with sufficient liquidity and no hidden position-size restrictions. The larger the selection, the better — more assets let traders adapt to changing market conditions instead of depending on one or two pairs.
5
Challenge Conditions
Before paying for a challenge, you should clearly understand the profit target, time limits, daily loss limit, maximum drawdown, and allowed instruments. If any of these details are missing from the public rules, ask before purchasing — not afterward.
6
Payout Speed
Review how often payouts can be requested, whether there is a minimum payout threshold, and the available withdrawal methods. Additionally, verify trader feedback on independent review platforms.

Red Flags: How to Spot a Scam Prop Firm
The crypto prop trading industry attracts both legitimate firms and questionable operators. Watch out for these warning signs before paying for a challenge.
1
Unclear Rules
If conditions are hidden in fine print or change after payment, walk away.
2
Unrealistic Promises
Statements such as “guaranteed income” do not reflect how real prop firms operate.
3
No Independent Reviews
Check platforms like Trustpilot and trader communities.
4
Excessively Restrictive Trading Rules
For example, banning overnight positions without a clear explanation.
5
Unclear Payout Process
A reputable prop firm clearly states the minimum payout threshold, payout schedule, and withdrawal methods.
Before starting a prop firm challenge, spend time reviewing these details.

What Is a Crypto Prop Firm?
−
A crypto prop firm provides traders with capital to trade cryptocurrency markets. The trader completes an evaluation challenge, receives a funded account, and shares profits according to a predefined profit split.

+

Why Is a One-Phase Challenge Better Than a Multi-Phase Challenge?
+
A one-phase challenge reduces the time required to reach a funded account. The trader completes a single evaluation instead of two or three stages, reducing both time and the cost of retakes.
What Is a Reasonable Daily Loss Limit?
+
Industry standards range from 4% to 6% of account balance per day. A 5% daily loss limit provides enough flexibility for trading while protecting capital from significant losses.
