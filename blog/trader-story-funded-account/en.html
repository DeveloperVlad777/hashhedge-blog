First Steps: Pyramid Schemes and Bitcoin at $320
Denis is a trader from Ukraine. His path into the financial world didn't begin with charts and strategies — it began with financial pyramid schemes. In 2014 he invested in Helix, a scheme that promised quick returns. The pyramid collapsed, the money was lost, but that was the moment Denis first heard about Bitcoin.
He bought BTC at $320 and ETH at $4. Not as an investor with a clear strategy — more out of curiosity. Over time the coins grew in value, and Denis got his first taste of how markets work. But the real trading journey still lay ahead.
The Accident and the Road to Trading
In 2017 Denis's life changed abruptly. A serious car accident left him bedridden for months. Doctors said he might remain paralysed. But Denis recovered — and the experience transformed his relationship with money and risk.
After his recovery he tried different business ventures: gravel sales, halva production, car rental (at one point he owned 8 cars). Each experience deepened his understanding of money and risk. But none of those businesses captivated him the way markets did.
Scalping, Losses, and a Friend's Money
Denis began trading actively. He became absorbed in scalping — a strategy that demands split-second decisions and nerves of steel. For a while it seemed like everything was going well. But markets have a way of punishing overconfidence.
Losses piled up. Denis blew his own capital, then borrowed from a friend. That money was lost too. It became a turning point — less financial than psychological. "Making money isn't the problem. The problem is keeping it," Denis says today.
That's when he understood: he needed a system. Not intuition, not luck — clear entry rules, strict risk management, and patience. He started looking for structure.

Advice for Beginning Traders
From his experience Denis has distilled several key principles:
1
One Strategy
Don't spread yourself across ten approaches. Pick one strategy and master it completely. Denis trades one method — and that's exactly what gave him consistency.
2
Backtest and Statistics
Before trading real money, test your strategy on historical data. Know your win rate, average risk-to-reward ratio, and maximum drawdown.
3
Risk Management is Everything
Maximum 0.5–1% risk per trade. No exceptions. Even if you're certain about a trade — the rule doesn't change. Breaking this rule is exactly what cost Denis his first challenge.
4
Don't Trade Out of FOMO
Fear of missing a trade is a trader's worst enemy. If the setup isn't perfect — skip it. The market will always give you another opportunity.
5
Preserving is More Important than Earning
Denis's core lesson: many people can make money, but very few can keep it. Protecting capital is the foundation of long-term success in trading.
