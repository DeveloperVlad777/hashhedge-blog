Doctors predicted a wheelchair. He ran a half-marathon.
At 20, Denis was diagnosed with herniated discs. Doctors gave bleak prognoses: if he walked at all, it would only be with a cane. Denis decided otherwise. He recovered, changed his lifestyle, and eventually ran a half-marathon.
"The doctor predicted a wheelchair, saying: if I walk at all, it'll only be with a cane. I proved him wrong. When I came back for a check-up six months later — he saw me and started crying. He said: 'He just really wanted to live.'"
This story matters because it explains who Denis is: someone who doesn't accept "no" as a final answer. Not from doctors, not from the market.
Crushed stone, halva, and 8 rented cars: a life of side hustles
Denis is from Belarus. A serial entrepreneur: sold CDs and phones, traded crushed stone and halva, ran a taxi fleet of eight cars, and flipped crashed Ladas.
But Denis admits that the classic "work for someone else" model isn't for him. What weighs on him isn't the work itself — it's the feeling of external obligation.
"What always gets me is that kind of obligation. Not the responsibility I put on myself — but what others demand of me."
Trading appealed to him for exactly that reason: working for yourself, using your own mind, no bosses.
Ponzi schemes and the path to crypto
Denis's first serious losses weren't from trading — they were from Ponzi schemes. He put money into companies promising stable returns, and lost. Multiple times.
Crypto came as a natural next step: after building up capital again, the question became — where to put it?
"I'd built up some capital and it had to go somewhere. Money can't just sit there."
He entered crypto during a pump. Bought in, saw it rise, sold at the first sign of fear — and missed the big run.
"I thought: wow, it's already up a lot, I don't want to lose it — sold."
Does he regret it? Humanly, yes. But no self-blame.
"What's the point of regrets? FOMO is real, I won't deny it. But I'm human, not a robot. I laugh about it with friends. My brother still teases me."
Returning to crypto and the path to prop trading
After a break, Denis returned to crypto. He started learning trading, watching content, studying the market. On YouTube, he came across one of the top crypto influencers. In one video, the influencer talked about Hash Hedge — a prop firm that provides capital for management.
The logic was simple: "Why lose your own money when you can invest a little and earn much more?"
That's how Denis got into prop trading.
The first challenge: failure in two days
Denis blew his first account in two days. The reason was simple — he hadn't read the rules fully. He hadn't understood the daily drawdown rule: he thought unrealized PnL didn't count.
"I thought: I won't close it, the PnL is unrealized, nothing bad will happen."
He was wrong. The account was blocked. He contacted support — they explained that unrealized PnL also counts toward the daily drawdown. It wasn't hidden — Denis just hadn't finished reading.
"Support really responds fast. If you have questions, don't hesitate — go straight to support."
The second challenge: 10x on invested capital
The second time, Denis approached it differently. He read everything. He reworked his strategy and risk management. He cut risk to 0.5% per trade, trading with a 1:3 risk-reward ratio.
"I reined it in. Cut risk to 0.5%, trading with a 1:3 risk-reward. Step by step."
He traded methodically. Without rushing. Set a take-profit — and went to sleep.
"Went to sleep. Woke up, the take-profit hit, the account became Funded."
The result: 10x on invested capital.
Denis's strategy and advice for prop trading beginners
Denis trades using his own strategy, developed through backtesting and live trading. His advice for beginners:
—
Learn one strategy — that's enough. Don't spread yourself thin;
—
Backtest it and practice on a demo account or paper trades;
—
Strictly follow your risk management;
—
Gambling is evil. "Don't try to find setups where there aren't any";
—
Don't rush. Getting consistent results can take six months, a year.
"Trading is hard, boring work. The only thing you can do fast in trading is lose. Getting rich isn't fast — it's forever."
"Making money has never been my problem. The problem is keeping it."
"The main problem is not losing. The market will always give you the chance to earn."
